By Simon Kemmere, 10798250

Data extracted from the following sources: Adult Education level and Average wage



Storytelling

Changes to the current ecosystems in our oceans are being detected (SOURCE). These changes are mainly due to overfishing, but the second biggest influence can be led back to the amount of plastic found in oceans. Considering reports from all over the world (SOURCE) there can only be one conclusion: the amount of plastic that can be found in the ocean has significantly increased. Fish, mammals and other living creatures in the oceans are being deformed via plastics or die after ingesting these plastics, either through choking or just of the quantity of ingested plastic. This(SOURCE NRC) pilot whale ate 8 kilogrammes of plastic and then wound up on a beach in Thailand. This report is part of a series, where it becomes clear that there's a certain urgency to solve this problem. To further help ignite this flame of urgency, this website will guide the reader through a series of visualisations concerning the production of plastic in several countries over the world, the amount of threatened fish species in some of these countries and what is being done over the years (2014-2016) by countries to decrease the amount of plastic in the oceans.
